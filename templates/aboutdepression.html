What is Depression ?


Depression is a widespread mental health condition that affects millions of people worldwide. It goes beyond occasional feelings of sadness and is characterized by persistent and intense emotions of despair, hopelessness, and a pervasive lack of interest or pleasure in daily activities. This mood disorder can impact various aspects of a person's life, including their thoughts, emotions, and behaviors.

Symptoms :


  • Persistent sadness
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in activities
  • Changes in sleep patterns
  • Fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Treatment :


  • Multifaceted approach
  • Psychotherapy, especially cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Medications, such as antidepressants
  • Lifestyle modifications: exercise, healthy diet, sufficient sleep

Other Mental Health Problem :


  • Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia) :

    Involves long-term, chronic symptoms that may not be as severe as those of major depressive disorder but can last for years. Individuals with dysthymia may experience periods of major depression.

  • Bipolar Disorder (Manic-Depressive Illness) :

    Involves episodes of depression alternating with periods of mania or hypomania. Mania is characterized by elevated mood, increased energy, and impulsive behavior.

  • Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD):

    Occurs seasonally, often in the winter months when there is less natural sunlight. Symptoms include low energy, irritability, and changes in sleep and appetite.

  • Cyclothymic Disorder:

    A chronic mood disorder with numerous periods of hypomanic symptoms and mild depressive symptoms. Less severe than bipolar disorder.
